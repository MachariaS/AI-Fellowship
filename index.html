AI Lab Fellowship Program

Equipping young talented Africans with AI skills and mindset to create innovative solutions for Africa's digital transformation

Program Overview

Mission

To equip young talented Africans with AI skills and mindset to create innovative solutions for existing IAN startups and develop new AI-powered ventures that contribute to Africa's digital transformation.

Duration

10 Weeks intensive program (2-3 days per week at Launchpad co-working space)

Target Participants

Recent graduates and young professionals entering the AI space

Program Structure

1. Selection Process - March 31, 2025

AI Hackathon Pipeline:

  • Host AI hackathons after every 2 months
  • Identify top performers & invite for the screening phase

Two-Week Screening Process:

  • Candidates work on a real-world scenario with limited access to products
  • Evaluation criteria: technical skills, problem-solving approach, collaboration ability, and innovative thinking
  • Use technical & non technical assessment committee e.g Pick project Leads

Final Selection:

  • 8-12 candidates per cohort based on performance of their pitch, potential & organization capacity
  • Select diverse background e.g one person could take product ownership
  • Last day used as onboarding day: Introduction to IAN, AI ethical considerations

Week 1: April 1-7, 2025

Scoping and Work Structures

  • Scoping of the AI solution and roadmap
  • Roles alignment and assignments
  • Mentor relationship & feedback system initiated

Week 2-6: April 8 - May 12, 2025

Development Phase

  • Hands-on project work with portfolio companies (2 days/week)
  • Technical workshops and skill development (1 day/week)
  • Weekly progress reviews and feedback sessions

Week 7-8: May 13-26, 2025

Solution Refinement

  • Prototype testing and iteration
  • User feedback integration from teams & actual users
  • Technical documentation

Week 9-10: May 27 - June 9, 2025

Finalization & Presentation

  • Final solution development
  • Pitch preparation
  • Demo Day preparation
  • Documentation completion
  • Integration into existing system

3. Mentorship Structure

Internal Technical Mentors:

  • IAN Senior Developers (1:4 mentor-to-fellow ratio)
  • Weekly structured sessions and on-demand support

External Mentor Network:

  • Industry experts in AI and relevant domains
  • Monthly masterclasses and review sessions

4. Resources & Support

Technical Infrastructure:

  • Cloud computing credits (AWS, GCP, or Azure)
  • Development environments and tools
  • Access to relevant APIs and datasets

Learning Resources:

  • Curated learning paths for different AI domains
  • Access to online courses and technical documentation
  • Internal knowledge base and best practices

Workspace:

  • Dedicated area within Launchpad coworking space (Lower Ground)
  • Trello/other tool for Project Management

5. Compensation & Benefits

Financial Support:

  • Monthly stipend (30,000-40,000 KES depending on experience)

Professional Benefits:

  • Full membership to Launchpad co-working space
  • Networking opportunities with ecosystem partners
  • Priority consideration for 1-year Innovation Fellowship

6. Post-Fellowship

Venture Track:

  • Fellows with promising standalone ideas enter Kinetic Program
  • Support to develop business models and seek funding

Talent Network:

  • All fellows remain within our alumni network with ongoing engagement e.g news letter, events e.t.c
  • Opportunity to mentor future cohorts
  • Access to other job opportunities within IAN

Success Metrics

Output Metrics

  • 2-3 AI solutions developed per quarter
  • Technical documentation and AI-First Thinking (knowledge transfer)

Impact Metrics

  • Revenue/cost impact of AI solutions for portfolio companies
  • User engagement metrics for implemented solutions
  • Improvement in efficiency/productivity metrics
  • Impact stories & attracting funding

Talent Development Metrics

  • Conversion to full time fellows/staff
  • Retention rates
